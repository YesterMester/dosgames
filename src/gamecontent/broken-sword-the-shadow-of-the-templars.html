History of 'Broken Sword'

The Broken Sword series began with Broken Sword: The Shadow of the Templars in 1996. Developed by Revolution Software, it quickly became a favorite among adventure gamers.

The game features a captivating story involving a lawyer named George Stobbart. He finds himself entangled in a mysterious conspiracy involving the Knights Templar. This unique blend of history and mystery captured players' imaginations.

In 1997, the sequel, Broken Sword II: The Smoking Mirror, was released. It continued the adventures of George and his companion, Nico Collard. The game refined gameplay mechanics and storytelling.

The series saw more titles over the years, including Broken Sword: The Sleeping Dragon (2003) and Broken Sword: The Angel of Death (2006). Each installment built on the original, introducing new puzzles and character developments.

In 2013, fans experienced a remastered version of the first game. The Director’s Cut added new scenes while keeping the original’s charm intact. Players had the option to play Broken Sword online for the first time, expanding its reach.

The series remains popular, leading to discussions about a sixth installment. Charles Cecil, the creator, has indicated interest in using modern technology, like AI, to enhance future remasters. Players are eager for new adventures while celebrating the legacy of this iconic series.
